What is a SMART Power Flow Controller (SPFC)?

A SPFC enhances power transmission controllability using functional requirements and cost-effective solutions. It combines the best of all power flow technologies — electronics-based or otherwise — to optimize grid performance.

Its main job is to independently control active and reactive power flows in electric transmission lines, maximizing efficiency and reliability.

Functional Requirements

Reduce reactive power within limits while maintaining voltage stability. Increase active power up to the thermal limits. The SPFC achieves this with independent power flow control.

Why It Matters

  • Reduces reactive power losses → increases efficiency
  • Reduces environmental impact
  • Frees up grid components for more active power
  • Redirects power to underutilized lines → reduces grid congestion
  • Delays costly new transmission lines

SMART Objectives

  • S – Specific: Meet utility needs
  • M – Measurable: Reliable, efficient, portable
  • A – Attainable: Realistic outcomes
  • R – Relevant: Power grid efficiency
  • T – Time-bound: Milestone-driven delivery

Sen Transformers (ST)

STs, placed at strategic locations, help control power flow without building new lines — reducing cost and environmental impact while improving power delivery efficiency.
